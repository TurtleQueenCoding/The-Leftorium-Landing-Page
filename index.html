Our Story

In 1991, our founder Ned Flanders said toodle-loo to the pharmaceutical game and decided to open up a store called The Leftorium at his local mall in Springfield. As a lefty himself, Ned knew from personal experience that being left-handed is not all peaches and cream. Most products are designed with the typical right-handed consumer in mind, and this makes daily tasks like writing in a notebook or driving a standard transmission needlessly frustrating for one out nine Americans. Inspired by his love for his community, which boasts a rate of left-handedness three times the national average, Ned created a one-stop shop for southpaws, selling both practical and lifestyle goods for the left-handed man, woman, and child.

Starting his own business was a huge personal risk, and Ned struggled to find a customer base at the beginning. He suffered tremendous financial loss, and the store was nearly closed forever until Ned's neighbor, Homer Simpson, rallied his community and told all the lefties he knew about the store. With Homer's help, Ned was able to bounce back. The store became incredibly successful, not only due to Springfield's need for left-handed products, but also due to Ned's caring customer service and magnanimous spirit towards his fellow southpaws.

Today, Ned is still operating the original Leftorium at the Springfield Mall. His sons Rod and Todd have expanded the franchise by opening branches in nearby Shelbyville and Capital City. With this online store, Ned can bring speciality left-handed goods not only to customers in Springfield and surrounding areas, but all over the world.
